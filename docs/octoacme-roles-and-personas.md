# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove team impediments, and coach the organization on process improvement. They serve as servants to the team and organization, enabling continuous delivery and high-performing teams.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove impediments and blockers that impede team progress
- Coach the team on agile practices and continuous improvement
- Monitor team velocity and help optimize sprint capacity
- Escalate organizational impediments beyond team control
- Protect the team from scope creep and external distractions

### Goals
- Enable high team velocity and sustainable pace
- Foster psychological safety and continuous learning
- Maximize team autonomy and self-organization
- Reduce cycle time and improve predictability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- One-on-ones with team members for coaching
- Collaboration with Project Manager on blockers and resource planning
- Documentation of impediments and improvement actions

### Key Interactions
- **With Project Manager**: Coordinates on timeline risks, escalations, and dependency management
- **With Developers**: Coaches on agile principles, removes technical blockers
- **With Product Manager**: Helps clarify requirements and acceptance criteria during planning
- **With QA/Testing**: Ensures testing is integrated into sprint workflow

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers translate product requirements into intuitive, user-centered designs. They collaborate closely with Product Managers to validate solutions and with Developers to ensure high-quality implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and analyze user needs
- Create user flows, wireframes, and high-fidelity designs
- Define interaction patterns and design systems
- Validate designs through user testing and feedback loops
- Collaborate with Developers on implementation feasibility
- Ensure accessibility and usability standards are met

### Goals
- Deliver delightful, usable user experiences
- Reduce friction and improve customer satisfaction
- Ensure design consistency across products
- Balance user needs with business constraints

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Manager and stakeholders
- Collaboration sessions with Developers during implementation
- User testing and feedback documentation
- Design system and pattern library maintenance

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Manager**: Translates customer insights into intuitive designs; validates design solutions against user needs
- **With Developers**: Partners on implementation approach; reviews code for design fidelity
- **With QA/Testing**: Works together to validate user experience in acceptance testing
- **With Stakeholder Champion**: Incorporates stakeholder feedback into iterative design refinement

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers own the release calendar, coordinate cross-team release activities, and ensure smooth deployments to production. They are the primary point of contact for major and minor releases.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain the release calendar and schedule
- Coordinate release planning across all teams (engineering, QA, documentation, support)
- Manage release checklists and deployment workflows
- Communicate release status and timelines to stakeholders
- Coordinate post-deployment verification and rollback if needed
- Document release notes and known issues

### Goals
- Execute predictable, low-risk releases
- Minimize downtime and customer impact
- Ensure clear communication across all stakeholders
- Enable rapid rollback if issues are detected

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings and kick-offs
- Cross-team coordination on deployment readiness
- Stakeholder updates and release announcements
- Post-release retrospectives and incident reviews

### Key Interactions
- **With Project Manager**: Aligns on release timing and manages dependencies
- **With Developers**: Ensures code is production-ready and deployment runbooks are complete
- **With QA/Testing**: Coordinates smoke tests and production validation
- **With Technical Writer**: Ensures release notes and user documentation are ready
- **With Stakeholder Champion**: Communicates release details and timelines to key stakeholders

---

## Stakeholder Champion

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Champions act as the primary liaison for specific stakeholder groups (e.g., Customer Support, Legal, Sales, Customer Success). They ensure stakeholder needs, feedback, and concerns are integrated throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Represent stakeholder interests and feedback in project planning and execution
- Gather and communicate stakeholder needs and constraints
- Facilitate communication between project team and stakeholder groups
- Review deliverables for stakeholder alignment and compliance
- Escalate stakeholder concerns and conflicting priorities
- Ensure stakeholder groups are informed of project progress and decisions

### Goals
- Maintain strong stakeholder alignment and satisfaction
- Prevent late-stage surprises and rework
- Enable stakeholder feedback to shape better outcomes
- Reduce post-release friction and support burden

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder interviews and feedback sessions
- Regular status updates to stakeholder groups
- Escalations and decision documentation
- Post-launch stakeholder reviews and feedback collection

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Manager**: Advocates for stakeholder constraints in prioritization and requirements
- **With Project Manager**: Escalates stakeholder concerns and manages stakeholder communication
- **With Release Manager**: Ensures stakeholder groups are prepared for releases
- **With UX Designer**: Incorporates stakeholder feedback into design refinement
- **Across all roles**: Serves as two-way communication bridge between stakeholders and project team

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers partner with Product and Engineering teams to create clear, comprehensive documentation. They document features, design decisions, user guides, and system architecture to support users, operators, and future maintainers.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain user-facing documentation and help content
- Document system design, architecture, and technical decisions
- Collaborate with Developers on API documentation and code samples
- Review product features for clarity and usability of documentation
- Maintain documentation accuracy and consistency across products
- Support localization and accessibility of documentation

### Goals
- Reduce support burden through clear, comprehensive documentation
- Enable users to self-serve and find answers quickly
- Preserve institutional knowledge for future teams
- Improve adoption and user satisfaction

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews with Product and Engineering
- User research and feedback on documentation clarity
- Collaboration on release notes and migration guides
- Documentation roadmap and maintenance planning

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Manager**: Understands feature goals and user needs; collaborates on user guide content
- **With Developers**: Partners on API documentation, code samples, and architecture documentation
- **With Release Manager**: Prepares release notes and migration documentation ahead of releases
- **With UX Designer**: Ensures documentation aligns with user interface and user flows
- **With Support/Customer Success**: Gathers feedback on documentation gaps and user questions

## Role Integration Matrix

The following matrix shows how roles interact during key project phases:

| Phase | Primary Owner | Key Collaborators | Support Roles |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Initiation** | Product Manager + Project Manager | Stakeholder Champion | Technical Writer (docs) |
| **Planning** | Project Manager + Product Manager | Scrum Master, UX Designer | Stakeholder Champion (alignment) |
| **Execution** | Developers + Scrum Master | Product Manager, UX Designer, QA | Technical Writer (docs), Stakeholder Champion |
| **Review & Testing** | QA/Testing + UX Designer | Developers, Product Manager | Stakeholder Champion (feedback) |
| **Release** | Release Manager | All roles (coordination) | Stakeholder Champion (comms) |
| **Post-Launch** | Product Manager (metrics) | Support/Customer Success | Technical Writer (doc updates) |

## Guidance for Adding Roles to Your Team

When expanding your team or adopting new roles:

1. **Clarify the gap**: Identify which existing process or outcome is suffering from the missing role.
2. **Define interactions**: Map how the new role intersects with existing roles and update collaboration patterns.
3. **Update documentation**: Add role descriptions to this document and reference them in project processes.
4. **Communicate clearly**: Ensure all team members understand the new role's purpose and how to work with it.
5. **Iterate**: Gather feedback after a few projects and refine role definitions as needed.
